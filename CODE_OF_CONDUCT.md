# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Agentmaster's community should be welcoming, safe, and useful to everyone who
participates in good faith. Maintainers and contributors are expected to treat
other people with dignity, communicate honestly, and make room for different
backgrounds, abilities, experiences, and viewpoints.

Participation includes code, documentation, issues, pull requests, reviews,
discussions, release coordination, and other spaces operated on behalf of the
project.

## Expected Behavior

Community members should:

- discuss ideas and technical decisions without attacking people;
- give and receive specific, constructive feedback;
- respect stated boundaries and requests to stop an interaction;
- acknowledge mistakes and make a reasonable effort to repair harm;
- credit other people's work and sources;
- protect confidential, personal, and security-sensitive information;
- keep promotion and off-topic material within appropriate boundaries;
- help maintain a productive environment for contributors at every experience
  level.

Disagreement is allowed. Hostility, intimidation, and bad-faith participation
are not.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not permitted:

- harassment, threats, stalking, or unwanted personal attention;
- insults, personal attacks, deliberate humiliation, or discriminatory remarks;
- sexualized language, imagery, or attention that is inappropriate for the
  project space;
- publishing or using another person's private information without permission;
- impersonation or evading a moderation decision through another identity;
- encouraging violence, self-harm, or other danger;
- sustained disruption, trolling, spam, or commercial promotion unrelated to
  the project;
- knowingly presenting another person's work as your own;
- retaliation against someone who raises a concern or participates in an
  investigation;
- other conduct that a reasonable person would understand as threatening the
  safety or continued participation of community members.

## Scope

This policy applies in all Agentmaster-managed community spaces. It also applies
when someone is officially representing Agentmaster, including through a project
account, official event, or communication made on the project's behalf.

Private conduct may be considered when it creates a credible safety risk or has
a direct and substantial effect on participation in the project.

## Reporting a Concern

Do not place sensitive incident details, private messages, personal information,
or evidence in a public issue.

Use a private contact method published by the repository maintainer when one is
available. If no private method is listed, open a minimal issue with the
`conduct` label or a `[Conduct] Private contact request` title. Include only a
request for private contact; do not describe the incident publicly.

A useful private report includes:

- what occurred and when;
- where the incident occurred;
- links, screenshots, or logs that can be shared safely;
- people involved and possible witnesses;
- any immediate safety or confidentiality concern;
- the outcome or support being requested.

Anonymous reports will be considered, although limited follow-up may make them
harder to investigate. Knowingly false or retaliatory reports may themselves
violate this policy.

## Handling Reports

Maintainers responsible for a report will:

- acknowledge it as soon as practical;
- protect the reporter's identity and evidence to the extent possible;
- disclose information only to people needed for a fair investigation or to
  meet a legal or safety obligation;
- give involved parties a reasonable opportunity to provide relevant context;
- avoid handling a report when they have a conflict of interest;
- communicate the outcome privately when appropriate.

No maintainer may investigate or decide a report made against themselves. When
no neutral project maintainer is available, an uninvolved third party should be
asked to review the matter.

## Enforcement

Responses depend on severity, impact, context, prior behavior, and the person's
willingness to correct the harm. Maintainers may skip steps when necessary to
protect the community.

1. **Correction** — A private explanation of the concern and the behavior
   expected going forward.
2. **Warning** — A formal warning with consequences for continued violations.
3. **Temporary restriction** — Time-limited restrictions on interactions,
   participation, or access to project spaces.
4. **Temporary suspension** — Removal from project spaces for a defined period,
   with conditions for returning.
5. **Permanent removal** — Permanent loss of access or participation when severe
   harm or repeated violations make continued participation unsafe.

Moderation actions may include editing or removing content, locking discussions,
closing contributions, limiting contact, revoking project roles, or reporting
conduct to the relevant platform or authorities when required.

## Appeals and Recordkeeping

A person affected by an enforcement decision may request one reconsideration by
providing new information, identifying a material process error, or explaining
why the response is disproportionate. The appeal should be reviewed by someone
who did not make the original decision whenever possible.

Maintainers should retain only the records needed to document the report,
decision, and enforcement history. Access should remain limited, and records
should not be kept longer than operational or legal needs require.
